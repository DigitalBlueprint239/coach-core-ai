# Brand Voice Designer

This skill helps businesses develop a distinct brand voice and create guidelines for maintaining it.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Define the Brand's Personality

- Review the brand's positioning, values, and target audience.
- Choose 3-5 brand personality traits that align with the brand (e.g., friendly, professional, witty, authoritative, playful).
- For each trait, describe what it is and what it is not (e.g., "Friendly, but not overly familiar. Witty, but not sarcastic.").

### Step 2: Develop the Brand Voice

- Based on the personality traits, define the brand's voice across the following dimensions:
    - **Tone:** What is the overall feeling you want to evoke (e.g., positive, optimistic, serious, urgent)?
    - **Rhythm & Pace:** Is the language fast-paced and energetic, or slow and thoughtful?
    - **Vocabulary:** What kind of words do you use (e.g., simple and accessible, or technical and sophisticated)?
    - **Grammar & Punctuation:** Are you formal and grammatically strict, or more conversational and relaxed?

### Step 3: Create Brand Voice Guidelines

- Develop a set of guidelines that explain how to apply the brand voice in different contexts (e.g., website copy, social media posts, customer service emails).
- Include "do's and don'ts" for each context.
- Provide examples of on-brand and off-brand communication.

### Step 4: Produce Deliverables

- Create a Markdown document titled `brand_voice_guide.md`.
- The guide should include:
    - The brand's personality traits.
    - A detailed description of the brand voice.
    - Clear guidelines and examples for applying the brand voice.

## Examples

### Example Input

"I want my financial advisory firm to sound more approachable and less intimidating. Can you help me create a new brand voice?"

### Example Output

A `brand_voice_guide.md` file that defines a new brand voice for the financial advisory firm. The voice might be described as "professional, yet friendly and reassuring." The guide would include examples of how to apply this voice in different scenarios, such as writing a blog post that explains a complex financial topic in simple terms, or responding to a client's email with empathy and understanding.
