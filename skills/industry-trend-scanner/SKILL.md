# Industry Trend Scanner

This skill identifies and analyzes industry trends to help businesses stay ahead of the curve.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Define the Industry

- Ask the user to specify their industry.
- If the user provides a broad industry, ask for a more specific niche (e.g., instead of "restaurants," ask for "fast-casual Italian restaurants").

### Step 2: Scan for Trends

- Use web search with queries like "[industry] trends 2026," "future of [industry]," and "[industry] consumer behavior changes."
- Consult reputable sources such as industry publications, market research reports, and news articles.
- Look for trends in the following areas:
    - **Technology:** New tools, software, or platforms that are changing the industry.
    - **Marketing & Sales:** New strategies and channels for reaching and converting customers.
    - **Consumer Behavior:** Changes in how customers discover, evaluate, and purchase products or services.
    - **Business Models:** New ways of creating, delivering, and capturing value.

### Step 3: Analyze and Synthesize

- For each identified trend, analyze its potential impact on the user's business.
- Categorize trends as short-term, medium-term, or long-term.
- Identify the opportunities and threats associated with each trend.

### Step 4: Produce Deliverables

- Create a Markdown document titled `industry_trend_report.md`.
- The report should include:
    - An executive summary of the key trends.
    - A detailed analysis of each trend, including its potential impact, opportunities, and threats.
    - Actionable recommendations for how the user can leverage positive trends and mitigate the risks of negative trends.

## Examples

### Example Input

"I own a small accounting firm. What are the latest trends I should be aware of?"

### Example Output

An `industry_trend_report.md` file that details the latest trends in the accounting industry, such as the adoption of AI-powered automation, the shift to cloud-based accounting software, and the growing demand for advisory services. The report would also provide recommendations on how the accounting firm can adapt to these trends, such as by investing in new technology, training its staff, and expanding its service offerings.
